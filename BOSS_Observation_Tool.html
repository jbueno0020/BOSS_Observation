BOSS Observation Tool - Help Guide

What is BOSS?

BOSS (Behavioral Observation of Students in Schools) is a systematic direct observation tool used to measure student engagement in the classroom using momentary time sampling.

Getting Started

  1. Enter student name, observer name, and select observation duration
  2. Click "Start Observation" to begin the timer
  3. Every 15 seconds, you'll be prompted to record behavior
  4. Observe and record for Target Student (4x) then Comparison Peer (1x)
  5. Take notes throughout the observation

Behavior Codes

On-Task Behaviors:

  • AET (Active Engaged Time): Actively participating in assigned activity
  • PET (Passive Engaged Time): Passively attending to assigned activity

Off-Task Behaviors:

  • OFT-M (Off-Task Motor): Physical movement not related to task
  • OFT-V (Off-Task Verbal): Verbal behavior not related to task
  • OFT-P (Off-Task Passive): Not engaged, not disruptive

Keyboard Shortcuts

  • Ctrl+1 - Record AET
  • Ctrl+2 - Record PET
  • Ctrl+3 - Record OFT-M
  • Ctrl+4 - Record OFT-V
  • Ctrl+5 - Record OFT-P
  • 1-5 - Same behaviors when not typing

Tips for Accurate Observations

  • Position yourself where you can see both students clearly
  • Record the behavior at the exact moment the beep sounds
  • Do not average behavior over the interval
  • Maintain objectivity - record what you see, not interpretations
  • Use notes to capture context and important details
